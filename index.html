Why care about wildlife?

The fact that life has managed to exist up to this point, surviving countless natural disasters and changes to atmospheric conditions, is a result of evolution over billions of years. It is the intricate relationship between the multitude of diverse species within an ecosystem that allows for life to persist against even the most unlikely odds.

With more biodiversity, more species will survive disturbances to their ecosystems, allowing for energy pathways to remain from producers to apex predators. This ensures the survival of the ecosystem, and hence this is called ecosystem resilience.

Did You Know?

Tigers are apex predators, meaning they play a crucial role in maintaining the balance of their ecosystems.

Without predators like tigers, prey populations can grow unchecked, leading to overgrazing and habitat destruction.

Wildlife conservation helps maintain biodiversity, which is essential for a healthy and sustainable planet.
