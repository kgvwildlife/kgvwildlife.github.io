Common birds near KGV

Black-collared starling

Gracupica nigricollis

The black-collared starling is a common bird species, found in both parks and urban areas throughout the year. They are characterised by their yellow spot around the eyes, their white head, and black collar.

Eurasian tree sparrow

Passer montanus

The Eurasian tree sparrow has a population of around 290,000 mature individuals in Hong Kong, making it one of the most common birds. They can be identified by their chestnut brown head, white cheek with a dot, and fluffy white underbelly.

Red-whiskered bulbul

Pycnonotus jocosus

The red-whiskered bulbul has a population of around 80,000 mature individuals in Hong Kong, and can be identified by their distinct red spot beneath their eyes, and their pointed black crest.

Oriental magpie

Pica serica

Oriental magpies can be spotted throughout the year, although they are more commonly seen between September to March. They can grow to be as large as 50cm, and their wings and tail have a slight blue-green glint.

Black kite

Milvus migrans

Black kites are more common during autumn and winter. They are often spotted near the harbour, as they scavenge for dead fish for food. However, they can also be found soaring above urban areas.

Feral pigeon

Columba livia domestica

Feral pigeons are often seen in large flocks in urban areas throughout Hong Kong. They have dark grey bodies with black streaks, sometimes with black or white patches.

Spotted dove

Spilopelia chinensis

Spotted doves are common resident birds in Hong Kong, found in parks, gardens, agricultural areas, and wooded areas. They are often seen on the ground, foraging for seeds, grains, and insects, and are usually found in pairs or small groups. They have a black neck covered in small white spots.

Blue whistling thrush

Myophonus caeruleus

The blue whistling thrush is commonly found in woodlands near streams, as well as in parks and urban areas. They are typically black or dark blue in colour, with pale blue spots on the head, breast, and wings. They also have a long, piercing whistling call.

How can we support the native birds of Hong Kong?

- It is generally not recommended to feed common urban birds like sparrows and pigeons in Hong Kong, because it can spread disease, create dependency, and attract rodents.

- Instead, it is better to plant native Hong Kong flora such as native hibiscus and gardenias which can provide nectar and attract insects that birds eat.

- Avoid leaving bright lights on at night, as light pollution can disorient birds and increase the number of bird-window collisions.
